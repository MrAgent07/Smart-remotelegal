Support

Beam Remote controls TVs on your home Wi‑Fi. Your iPhone and TV must be on the same network — not a guest or isolated VLAN.

Quick tip: If discovery fails, disable VPN, confirm Local Network permission is on, and try Reconnect from the remote screen.

Requirements

  • iPhone or iPad running iOS 17 or later
  • TV and phone on the same Wi‑Fi network
  • Local Network permission enabled for Beam Remote in iOS Settings

Getting started

  1. 1 Install Beam Remote from the App Store.
  2. 2 Allow Local Network access when prompted.
  3. 3 Wait for your TV in the discovery list, or enter its IP manually.
  4. 4 For Google TV / Android TV, enter the pairing code shown on your TV.

Supported platforms

Roku / TCL Roku

Remote, keyboard, and app launcher. No pairing code required.

Google TV / Android TV

Pairing, remote, app shortcuts, and keyboard when a TV text field is focused.

Samsung Smart TV

Pairing and remote. Feature set varies by model and firmware.

LG webOS

Pairing and remote. Feature set varies by model and firmware.

Troubleshooting

  • Confirm TV and phone are on the same Wi‑Fi (disable VPN while testing).
  • iOS Settings → Beam Remote → enable Local Network.
  • Use Reconnect on the remote screen or Forget TV in Settings, then pair again.
  • Google TV: pairing uses port 6467; remote control uses port 6466. Both must be reachable on your LAN.
  • Keyboard: focus a search or text field on the TV, dismiss the on-screen keyboard on the TV, then send text from the app.
  • Apps tab: tap a shortcut to open Netflix, YouTube, etc. on Google TV (app must be installed on the TV).

App Store listing

The app may appear as Beam Premium Remote on the App Store. The name on your home screen is Beam Remote.
